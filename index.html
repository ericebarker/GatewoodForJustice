Forward Together! Rev. Curtis E. Gatewood’s lifetime of involvement in the NAACP began at just 10 years old. An actively involved youth participant, he went on to serve in leadership positions at the local, state and national levels. He now wants to serve as NC NAACP president with the goal of reinvigorating North Carolina branches from the ground up, while providing oversight and accountability at the state level.

  • National NAACP Initiatives: One Nation Working Together – NC State Director; America’s Journey for Justice – NC State Coordinator
  • NC NAACP: 2nd Vice President; District 8 Director; Direct Action Committee Chair; Interim Director for Field & Branch Operations
  • Historic Thousands on Jones Street (HKonJ): Mobilization Committee Chair; HKonJ Coalition Coordinator 2011-2017
  • Branch Presidency: 8 years Durham NAACP President

Proven leadership from the ground up.

Curtis E. Gatewood has dedicated his life in serving the NAACP and fighting for justice. A lifetime member of the NAACP, Rev. Gatewood has been active in the organization since the age of 10. He has tirelessly devoted himself to critical issues across North Carolina: criminal justice; police misconduct and violence; systemic inter-community violence; educational disparities affecting black and brown youth; use of hate speech as a political tool; and voter access, education, and engagement. Rev. Curtis Gatewood wants to use his decades of experience of leadership and action to create a united vision for the NC NAACP, one which embodies the ideal of “leadership from the ground up.”

Background

The Rev. Curtis Everette Gatewood grew up in Wadesboro, NC, one of 14 children born to the Rev. Dr. Wade H. Gatewood and Geneva Gaddy Gatewood. A card-carrying member of the NAACP at age 10, Rev. Gatewood was an active participant in the local NAACP youth chapter growing up. Rev. Gatewood holds an associate degree in early childhood development from Durham Technical Community College. He attended Livingstone College, in Salisbury, NC, as a business administration major, but ultimate went on to complete his undergraduate studies at the Apex School of Theology (ASOT) with a Bachelor of Theology degree. An African American theology college, ASOT also provided Rev. Gatewood with graduate-level coursework in Christian counseling. Rev. Gatewood was ordained by the County Line Baptist Association through the Penn Avenue Missionary Baptist Church in Oxford, NC, by Pastor Rev. Willie S. Darby who also served the president of the local NAACP chapter. Rev. Gatewood’s professional career includes expertise in management, leadership, and fiscal responsibility. His early career included positions in banking at Durham’s Mechanics & Farmers Bank (M&F Bank) and in retail management. He went on to spend over 10 years as an early childhood education administrator at Bright World Child Development Centers, which involved direction of three different childcare centers. He has also held the staff position of HKonJ Coordinator with the NC NAACP. Rev. Gatewood has been married to his wife, Odessa Burnette Gatewood, since 1988. The couple have two children, Desmera Gatewood and Anthony Hawkins.

Involvement and Activism

In 1994, while engaging in street ministry in Durham, NC, a neighborhood child of only two years old was killed by a stray bullet. In response, Rev. Gatewood spearheaded the “1,000 Man March Against Violence & Self-Hatred,” the largest march against inner-city violence in the city’s history. Following the march, the neighborhood received attention and assistance from local governmental officials and Gatewood emerged as a prominent local community leader.

Branch Presidency He went on to become one of the youngest members to be elected as president of the Durham branch of the NAACP, where he served from1995 to 2003. Rev. Gatewood’s presidential campaign alone recruited nearly 150 new members, mainly young adults, for the Durham NAACP. Gatewood’s emboldened leadership, formidable recruitment efforts, and outspoken attention to such issues as education and police misconduct garnered significant attention. In 1996, the branch was named NC NAACP Branch of the Year and received the award for Highest Membership Increase Percentage in the Southeast Region. Gatewood himself was awarded NC NAACP President of the Year.

Historic Thousands on Jones Street (HKonJ) In 2005, Rev. Gatewood ran for and was elected 2nd Vice President of the NC NAACP State Conference. It was there that he began work with then-NC NAACP President Rev. Dr. William J. Barber, II. In 2006, he was appointed as the Historic Thousands On Jones Street (HKonJ) Mobilization Committee Chair. Gatewood served in this position until 2012, when he took on a staff position as HKonJ coordinator. During Gatewood’s first year in the position, mobilization surpassed 10,000 marchers for the first time. Then in 2014 and again in 2017, the total number of marchers surpassed 70,000 marchers (though some estimates believe the number may be closer to 100,000).

Moral Mondays On April 25, 2013, Rev. Curtis Gatewood was the first to be arrested during civil disobedience at the NC General Assembly, with N.C. NAACP President William J. Barber, II, and other NC NAACP leaders. The total of 17 ultimately arrested on this day would go on to serve as the kickoff for the Moral Monday movement. Throughout April of that year, Gatewood led NC Forward Together Local Organizing Tours across the western region of the state to generate support during the embryonic stages of the Moral Monday movement, while N.C. NAACP President Barber led local tours across the eastern region of the state.

State and National Involvement During his tenure in the Durham NAACP, Rev. Gatewood took on several leadership roles at the state level. He was appointed as N.C. NAACP Direct Action Committee Chair in 1995 and appointed as N.C. NAACP District 8 Director in 1997. While working with the NC NAACP, Rev. Gatewood held the position of North Carolina State Director for the One Nation Working Together initiative, jointly sponsored by the National NAACP & AFL-CIO. This role culminated in a mass mobilization held at National Mall in DC in October of 2010. In 2015, Gatewood served as North Carolina State Coordinator for the America’s Journey for Justice initiative, sponsored by the National NAACP. Rev. Gatewood took on a position as Interim NC NAACP Director for Field & Branch Operations in 2016. Rev. Gatewood resigned his position with NC NAACP in 2017 in order to seek an NC NAACP elected office. At that time, he bore the distinction of being the longest N.C. NAACP staff member to serve during the NC NAACP Barber Administration.
